Fun facts

Once the Capital of Norway: Before Oslo took the crown, Bergen was the capital of Norway in the 12th and 13th centuries. The famous royal Bergenhus Fortress dates all the way back to the 1200s,Run by German Merchants: For centuries, the city's trade was completely controlled by the Hanseatic League, a powerful commercial and defensive confederation of merchant guilds. German merchants actually ran the historic Bryggen wharf, and many local families still have German surnames like Friele and Mowinckel. UNESCO Heritage on Thin Wood: The colorful wooden buildings of the Bryggen Wharf are a UNESCO World Heritage site. Because the city was built almost entirely of wood, it has burned down—or suffered massive fires—more than 15 times. Bryggen Wharf Bergenhus Fortress Hanseatic League
